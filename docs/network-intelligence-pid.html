Product Idea Document · Job Agent

Network Intelligence System

A targeting framework for job search outreach — WHO to reach, WHERE to find them, WHEN to message, and WHY they'll reply.

Status: Pre-build Author: HK + Claude Date: April 2026 Repo: harrykapoor19/job-agent
01

The Core Problem

Cold outreach in job search fails almost universally. Not because of bad writing — but because of bad targeting. Most people do one of two things:

❌ The Fool's Move

Message Sam Altman at 3pm

  • 10,000+ DMs/week
  • Social media assistant filters
  • Zero hiring authority
  • Reply rate: effectively 0%
  • Famous = inbox overload
⚠️ Mid Approach

Message the Hiring Manager

  • Correct role, but everyone does this
  • Still gets 50-200 msgs/week
  • Feels transactional
  • Reply rate: 5-15%
  • Saturated channel
✅ The Smart Move

Message the "Hidden Gem"

  • Relevant to the role
  • Gets <10 msgs/week
  • Growth mindset, eager to help
  • Reply rate: 40-70%
  • Can champion you internally
Core Insight

The goal is not to reach the most important person. The goal is to reach the most reachable relevant person. Someone who has hiring influence (or a path to it), is not famous enough to be overwhelmed, and is in a mindset where they want to make connections.

02

Framework I — WHO to Target

We score every person at a target company on 6 factors. The final "Target Score" determines whether to reach out and in what order.

The 6 Targeting Factors

1. Role Relevance High weight

Are they in the team you'd join? Do they work on the product area you care about? Can they vouch for you or flag your application internally?

signal: title keywords, team name,
LinkedIn "About" section, recent posts

2. Fame / Inbox Load High weight

Inverted metric — lower is better. Twitter followers, LinkedIn followers, speaking appearances, press mentions. Sam Altman = 0 points. A PM Lead with 800 followers = high points.

signal: follower count (Twitter, LinkedIn),
# of press mentions in last 6 months

3. Growth Mindset Signal High weight

Do they post about helping, building, learning? Do they engage with strangers? Do they respond to replies on their posts? This predicts reply-likelihood more than anything.

signal: reply-to-post ratio on Twitter,
keywords: "hiring", "DMs open", "happy to chat"

4. Company Tenure Medium weight

Newer employees (<18 months) are more network-hungry. They're still building their internal credibility and external reputation. They want to make useful connections.

signal: LinkedIn "joined [company] X ago",
+30 pts if <12 months, +15 pts if 12-24 months

5. Profile Match to You Medium weight

Shared background (same industry, similar career arc, same school) creates instant rapport. Someone who was also a PM at a fintech → AI company gets it immediately.

signal: career overlap, sector overlap,
same previous companies, shared connections

6. Network Distance Medium weight

2nd degree (you know someone who knows them) is significantly better than 3rd degree or cold. Even a weak tie is enough to justify mentioning the mutual.

signal: LinkedIn mutual connections,
2nd = +25 pts, 3rd = +10 pts, cold = 0 pts

Composite Target Score

Weighted sum of all 6 factors, normalized to 0–100:

Output: Ranked List

For every target company, the system returns a ranked shortlist of 3-5 people, sorted by Target Score. Top person = first outreach. You message them one at a time, not all at once.

Framework II — WHERE to Reach Them

Each person has a primary platform where they're most reachable. The system detects this automatically based on their public activity signals.

Channel Best for Detection signal Reply rate Use when
Twitter / X reply Technical founders, researchers, builders Posts >2x/week, replies to strangers High (30-60%) They're active; reply to their post within 10 min
LinkedIn DM PMs, operators, recruiters, execs Active LinkedIn poster, recently connected with others Medium (15-35%) Professional tone fits, they're not on Twitter
Email (cold) Technical leads, researchers, anyone with published email Published on website, GitHub, paper, newsletter Medium (10-25%) Twitter + LinkedIn both cold; email adds formality
Substack comment Thinkers, writers, content-heavy people Active Substack, comments enabled High (20-40%) Underused; authors notice comments deeply
LinkedIn connection note Anyone with LinkedIn presence LinkedIn exists Medium (10-30%) Low-friction start; personalized note on connect request

Channel Priority Logic

Priority Order

1. Warm path first — if you have a mutual connection, ask them for an intro before doing anything else.

2. Their most active channel second — where do they actually post and reply? Go there.

3. Twitter reply hack — if they're on Twitter, this is usually the highest-leverage play. Engage on their content first, then DM.

4. LinkedIn as fallback — if nothing else works, it always exists. But it's more saturated.

5. Email as escalation — only after Twitter/LinkedIn attempts, or if they have published their email.

04

Framework III — WHEN to Message

Timing is the difference between being seen and being buried. The system tracks posting patterns to predict the best window.

The 10-Minute Twitter Hack — Productized

The Hack

When a founder or key person posts on Twitter, the first 10-15 minutes is when they're staring at their phone watching replies come in. This is the single highest-attention window. A thoughtful reply in this window has a 10-20x higher chance of being seen and responded to vs. a cold DM 3 days later.

How We Productize This

1

Build Posting Pattern Model

Pull last 30-50 tweets from each target person. Compute: avg post time, day-of-week frequency, time zone inference, avg gap between posts. Most US founders post Mon-Wed, 7-9am their local time (phone in bed habit).

2

Predict Next Post Window

From the pattern, predict: "Tom Brown typically posts Tuesday-Thursday between 8-9am PT. Next likely post window: Tuesday morning." The system shows you this as a scheduling recommendation.

3

Real-Time Monitor + Push Notification

Scheduler polls their Twitter every 15-20 minutes during the predicted window. When a new post is detected, macOS push notification fires: "Tom Brown just posted — reply window open. Click to see tweet."

4

Reply Draft Ready

Claude pre-generates a reply draft based on: (a) the tweet content, (b) your profile and interest in their company, (c) communication style match. You review, tweak in 60 seconds, post. Then follow up with a DM.

5

DM After Engagement

After replying to 2-3 of their posts over 1-2 weeks, send the DM. You're no longer cold — you're someone they've seen before. This is the warm → warm-er path without a mutual connection.

General Timing Rules by Channel

ChannelBest timeWorst timeWhy
Twitter reply Within 10 min of their post 2+ hours after post They're watching notifications live
Twitter DM Right after a reply they liked/responded to Cold DM with no prior engagement Context and recognition drive replies
LinkedIn DM Tue-Thu, 8-11am or 6-8pm their TZ Monday morning, Friday afternoon Professional mode — not commute, not weekend

Framework IV — WHY They'll Reply

The message content has to match the person. Claude generates the outreach draft based on a "reply motivation" model — what makes this specific person want to respond?

Person TypeWhat they respond toWhat kills the message
Builder / Founder Specific insight about their product, genuine curiosity about what they're building, offer to help Generic "I love your company", asking for a job directly, too long
PM / Operator Shared problem they've worked on, relevant experience that's directly applicable, comp/role fit signal Vague profile, no specific role mentioned, flattery without substance
Researcher / Technical Evidence you've read their work, sharp question about their research, specific technical POV Business-speak, "I'm passionate about AI", lack of technical depth
New-to-company employee "I noticed you just joined X — how's the transition going?" Network-building framing, not job-asking Asking for referral immediately — they don't have leverage yet
Growth mindset poster Responding directly to something they wrote, adding a concrete insight, being useful Making it about you too soon; not referencing their content
The Golden Rule

The message should look like you've done 10 minutes of research on them specifically — not like you sent 50 people the same thing. Claude generates this using their recent tweets, LinkedIn summary, and any recent interviews. The personality-profiler skill feeds directly into the message generator.

System Design — What We're Building

1. CLI Chat Interface

cli.py

Main way you interact with the agent. Natural language: "add Anthropic to my list", "who should I reach at Cohere", "profile Tom Brown", "when should I message him". Powered by Claude with tool calls into the pipeline.

2. Target Company Intelligence

agent/pipeline.py (extended)

Tell the agent a company name → it runs job discovery, scores roles, gives a directional brief: what the company does, which team has PM roles, what type of PM they hire. Adds to Supabase companies table.

3. Network Path Finder

agent/network.py (new)

Given a company: finds relevant people using Apify LinkedIn scraper. Scores them on the 6 targeting factors. Returns a ranked list with warm/cold path, suggested channel, and suggested timing.

4. Reach-Out Intelligence

agent/reach_out.py (new)

For a specific person: deep research on their presence. Best channel (Twitter/LinkedIn/email/Substack). Twitter posting pattern + predicted next post time. Claude-generated outreach draft tuned to their communication style.

5. Twitter Activity Monitor

agent/twitter_monitor.py (new)

Polls Twitter for a watchlist of handles every 20 min during their predicted active window. macOS push notification within 10 min of a new post. Pre-generates reply draft. Tracks engagement history per person.

6. Profile (HK)

profile/harry.md (new)

Your resume, voice, strongest stories, career arc. Fed into every Claude prompt — scoring, outreach drafts, reply generation. Currently Rachita's profile; needs to be swapped to yours.

Data Flow

1

You add a company

python cli.py → "add Anthropic to my target list" → pipeline runs, jobs found and scored, company added to Supabase.

2

Company brief generated

Claude generates directional brief: company type, active roles, which team, culture signals. Pulls from job descriptions + web research.

3

Network path analysis

Apify scrapes people at the company on LinkedIn. Claude scores each on the 6 targeting factors. Returns ranked list: "Here are 4 people to reach. Start with [Person A] — they're a PM Lead, joined 8 months ago, active on Twitter, posts about product strategy."

4

Deep profile + reach-out intel

"Profile Person A" → personality profiler runs + reach_out.py adds: best channel = Twitter, posts Tue-Thu 8am PT, last post was 3 days ago, predicted next post: Tuesday. Draft reply ready.

5

Twitter monitor activated

Person A added to watchlist. Scheduler polls their Twitter every 20 min on Tuesday morning. You get a macOS notification: "Person A just posted → reply window open." Draft ready. You post within 10 min.
